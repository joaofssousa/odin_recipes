Carbonara Italiana

Carbonara is a Italian dish, it´s origin goes back to 1944, where it was founded in Rome.

A fusion from bacon with powered eggs, mostly american, with pasta and pecorino cheese, italian ingredients.

Ingredients:

  • 350g spaghetti
  • 100g pecorino cheese
  • 200g bacon
  • 1 egg + 2 egg yolks
  • 2 garlic
  • salt and pepper
  • olive oil

Steps:

  1. Beat the egg and egg yolks, and season with a little freshly ground pepper. Add the Pecorino cheese, mix, and set aside.
  2. Bring a pot of salted water to a boil and cook the pasta until al dente, according to the package instructions.
  3. Add a bit of olive oil to a large frying pan and fry the bacon and crushed garlic over medium heat until golden, about 5 minutes. Remove the garlic from the pan.
  4. rain the pasta (reserving a little of the cooking water) and place it in the pan with the bacon so it absorbs the flavor. Mix well. Add a bit of the cooking water, about 7 tablespoons, and toss to combine.
  5. Remove the pan from the heat and quickly add the egg and cheese mixture. Lift and toss the pasta with a fork or tongs until it’s fully coated. Season with salt if desired.
  6. Serve immediately with a bit more grated Parmesan cheese and black pepper.
